The founding of Rome

Traditionally, ancient Romans explain the origin of their city in terms of myth and legend. It is said that Romulus and Remus, the twins who were suckled by a she-wolf, were the founders of the city. They decided to build a city, but after an argument, Romulus killed his brother and the city took his name. According to the Roman annalists, this happened on 21 April 753 BC.
